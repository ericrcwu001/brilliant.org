## Hook (the bet)
"One step priced this call at **10** (L4). Now cut the *same* move into **two** half-steps — same u=6/5, d=4/5, R=1 each. What's the fair 2-step price?" The tempting answers — "still 10, more steps change nothing" and "just average the three terminal payoffs" — are both wrong. The bet motivates the discovery that pricing a tree is the **one-step fold, iterated**: roll the option value back layer by layer, q-weighting each branch. (The answer is 11, not 10 and not 44/3.)

## Core promise (one idea)
**Price a multi-step option by folding the tree backward — q-weight each step, discount by R, repeat.** The terminal nodes carry Pascal-counted weights `nCk·q^k(1−q)^(n−k)` (they sum to 1), and as the steps shrink to zero this discrete machine converges to the **Black-Scholes** curve — which we *reach* with a slider but **never grade**, because `N(·)` is irrational.

## EXACT-RATIONAL / N(·) CONSTRAINT (lesson-critical — the central design risk)
**Black-Scholes `c = S·N(d1) − K·e^(−rT)·N(d2)` and the continuous Greeks (Δ=N(d1), Γ, vega, Θ) are irrational** — `N(·)` is the standard-normal CDF, with **no rational closed form** (and CRR `u=e^(σ√Δt)`, `R=e^(rΔt)` are irrational exponentials). This lesson **grades ONLY the exact discrete binomial core**: the terminals (144 / 96 / 64), the risk-neutral node weights `nCk·q^k(1−q)^(n−k)` (1/4, 1/2, 1/4), the multi-step price (11), and the path counts (1, 2, 1) — **all rational** because u=6/5, d=4/5, R=1 ⇒ q=1/2 are chosen rational.

**Black-Scholes and the continuous Greeks are DISPLAY-ONLY.** They appear *only* via `optionBoard display:'greeksSlider'` / `slider` as the n→∞ / continuous-σ limit; any number printed (e.g. "≈ 10.45", N(d1)) is a **sourced reference** passed through a `ratToNumber`-style escape — **NEVER an `accept`**, and excluded from `validate-fixtures` goldens.

> **This is the exact analogue of `lesson-covariance-4`.** cov-4 grades **ρ² (rational)** while keeping the irrational **ρ = 1/√2 display-only**. Here the **binomial price is the "ρ²"** (the exact, graded object) and **Black-Scholes is the "ρ"** (the irrational, display-only object you reach but never grade). Same discipline: grade the rational object the engine reproduces; show the irrational limit, never accept it.

## Misconceptions (per-option refutation copy — `byOption`, feed-forward)
**Bet `prediction` ("split the 1-step (price 10) into 2 steps — what's the 2-step price?")** — 3 options, one correct:
- **(e)** "≈ 14.7 — average the three terminal payoffs (44+0+0)/3" → *"Let's test it — a plain average uses weights 1/3, 1/3, 1/3. The risk-neutral weights are **1/4, 1/2, 1/4** (Pascal 1-2-1 over 2²), and you must discount by Rⁿ (here R=1, so no discount). The middle node is twice as likely as each corner, so 1/4·44 = **11**, not 44/3."* (false)
- **(b)** "44 — weight the terminals by their path counts 1, 2, 1: 1·44 + 2·0 + 1·0" → *"Let's test it — those are raw **counts**, not probabilities; they sum to 4, not 1. Divide by 2ⁿ = 4 (equivalently multiply by q^k(1−q)^(n−k)): 44/4 = **11**. A count becomes a probability only after you normalize."* (false)
- "≈ 11 — re-fold the same q-weighted, discounted average one layer at a time" → *"Good instinct — pricing a tree is the one-step fold, iterated: C_u=22, C_d=0, then today = ½·22 + ½·0 = **11** (#17)."* (true)

**`opt5-model` (introduces the weight)** — refutes **(a)**: *"The node weight is `nCk·q^k(1−q)^(n−k)`, **not** `nCk·q^k`. Drop the `(1−q)^(n−k)` factor and the weights stop summing to 1 — they must (it's a probability distribution over terminals). Check: 1/4 + 1/2 + 1/4 = 1. ✓"*

**`opt5-gate` (which-method, label-stripped)** — options are DISPLAY labels positionally aligned with `optionMethods`; foils are **exactly** `⊂ CONFUSABLE[binomial-pricing]`:
- **Backward induction** (`backward-induction`) → *"Right *shape* — you do fold from the end. But bare backward induction doesn't carry the **risk-neutral q-weights** and the **1/R discount**; binomial pricing is that fold's q-weighted, discounted specialization."* (the near-miss — misconception **(d)**)
- **First-step analysis** (`first-step-analysis`) → *"That conditions **forward** on the first move and solves a recurrence. Here you fold **backward** from expiry — the values are known at the leaves, not the root."*
- **Recursion / self-reference** (`recursion-self-reference`) → *"Naive recursion re-expands the whole tree (and double-counts the recombining middle node). The structured q-weighted fold visits each node **once**."*
- **Binomial pricing** (`binomial-pricing`, correct) → *"Yes — the q-weighted backward fold over the lattice, discounting by R at each step. `gate.correct = binomial-pricing`."* (true)

**`opt5-bs-limit` (display-only)** — refutes **(c)**: *"As the steps shrink, the binomial price converges to Black-Scholes `c = S·N(d1) − K·e^(−rT)·N(d2)`. But `N(·)` is the normal CDF — **irrational, no rational closed form** — so the BS number you see (≈ 10.45) is a **sourced reference**, not a graded answer. The price this lesson grades is the **binomial** one (11). (Exactly like grading ρ² instead of the irrational ρ.)"*

**`opt5-mastery` Part B (count vs weight)** — refutes **(b)** as the spaced re-test: most likely wrong = giving the **count (2)** where the **weight (1/2)** is asked (or vice-versa). *"The middle node has **C(2,1)=2** paths (ud, du) — a count. Its **probability** is `C(2,1)·q(1−q) = 2·¼ = 1/2` — the count times `q^k(1−q)^(n−k)`. Count answers 'how many ways'; weight answers 'how likely'."*

**Misconceptions targeted (a–e):** (a) drops `(1−q)^(n−k)` (weights must sum to 1) — *model*; (b) "weight = the path count `nCk`" — *bet + mastery Part B* (the interleave target); (c) "Black-Scholes is exact / gradeable" — *bs-limit* (the N(·) constraint); (d) "multi-step needs a brand-new method" — *gate* (the `backward-induction` near-miss); (e) "price = plain average of terminal payoffs" (forgets Rⁿ-discount + q-weights) — *bet + win*.
