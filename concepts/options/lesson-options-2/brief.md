## Hook (the bet)
Same stock at S=100, same strike K=100, same expiry, r=0 ‚Äî and the call trades at $10. Is the put's price *pinned*, or do you need a volatility model to find it? Then the twist: at K=95 you see C=$8 and P=$2 ‚Äî the call costs $6 more than the put, but the stock is only $5 above the strike. Is that extra dollar **free money**? The bet forces the learner to admit they're reaching for "a pricing model" when the answer is forced by a single no-arbitrage identity ‚Äî and that a $1 mismatch is a literal arbitrage you can harvest.

## Core promise (one idea)
**Two portfolios with the same expiry payoff must cost the same ‚Äî so a call, a put, the stock, and a discounted strike are locked by one identity: `C ‚àí P = S ‚àí K¬∑D` (r=0 ‚áí `C ‚àí P = S ‚àí K`).** When a quote breaks the identity the gap *is* an arbitrage; the conversion trade harvests it. No Black-Scholes, no œÉ ‚Äî just no free lunch.

## Misconceptions (per-option refutation copy)

**Bet `opt2-bet` (byOption, ungraded ‚Äî "K=95, C=8, P=2, r=0: is the extra $1 free money?")** ‚Äî 3 options, one correct:
- "Yes ‚Äî parity forces C‚àíP to equal S‚àíK=$5, but the quotes give $6, so there's a risk-free edge." ‚Üí *"Right ‚Äî the gap (C‚àíP)‚àí(S‚àíK)=6‚àí5=1 is a genuine $1 arbitrage. The explore builds the trade that harvests it, and the mastery proves it nets +$1 today, $0 at expiry (#7)."* (**true**)
- "No ‚Äî you can't call it mispriced without a volatility / Black-Scholes model." ‚Üí *"Let's test it ‚Äî parity is an **exact no-arbitrage identity**, not an empirical fit. Two portfolios with the same expiry payoff must cost the same; no œÉ and no Black-Scholes enter. The mismatch is real (#7)."* (false ‚Äî misconception **(a)**)
- "No ‚Äî C‚àíP should equal K‚àíS = ‚àí$5, and these quotes look close enough." ‚Üí *"Sign check: a long call + short put pays `S_T‚àíK` at expiry (it synthesizes stock-minus-strike), so `C‚àíP = S‚àíK¬∑D`, **not** `K‚àíS`. Verify the payoff at S_T‚â•K and at S_T<K (#5)."* (false ‚Äî misconception **(b)**)

**Which-method gate `opt2-gate` (byOption, graded ‚Äî label-stripped; `correct=put-call-parity`; foils ‚äÇ `CONFUSABLE[put-call-parity]`):**
- **Put‚Äìcall parity** *(correct)* ‚Üí *"One identity ties C, P, S and K¬∑D; the missing quote is a single rearrangement. Fastest path ‚Äî you don't rebuild anything."*
- **No-arbitrage replication** *(foil ‚Üí misconception **(d)**)* ‚Üí *"You *could* rebuild the replicating portfolio leg-by-leg from scratch ‚Äî but the parity identity **already encodes** that replication. Recognizing the identity is the faster method here; replication is the long way to the same number."*
- **Payoff construction** *(foil)* ‚Üí *"Drawing/composing the payoff diagram gives the value **at expiry**, not the price relationship **today**. Parity is the cost identity that pins the fourth quote ‚Äî a payoff read alone won't."*

**`opt2-win` hint ladder** (accept `10`): nudge *"the identity is handed to you ‚Äî C‚àíP=S‚àíK at r=0"* ‚Üí method *"rearrange: P = C‚àí(S‚àíK) = 10‚àí(100‚àí100)"* ‚Üí answer *"**P=10** (#6). Notice you never needed a model ‚Äî no-arbitrage alone pinned it."* Most-likely wrong: stalling for "more info / a volatility number" (misconception (a)).

**`transfer-heldout` hint ladder** (accept `13`): nudge *"same identity, but now the discount bites: C = P + S ‚àí K¬∑D"* ‚Üí method *"K¬∑D = 44¬∑(10/11) = 40, so C = 3 + 50 ‚àí 40"* ‚Üí answer *"**C=13** (#8)."* Most-likely wrong: **`9`** ‚Äî ignoring D and using K=44 (`3+50‚àí44`), i.e. misconception **(c)** resurfaced on a fresh surface where it finally matters.

**`opt2-mastery` (split)** (accept gap `1`, profit-today `1`, profit-at-expiry `0`): Part A *"gap = (C‚àíP)‚àí(S‚àíK) = (8‚àí2)‚àí(100‚àí95) = 6‚àí5 = **1**."* Part B *"the scale tips toward the call side, so **sell the call, buy the put, buy the stock, borrow 95**; today: +8‚àí2‚àí100+95 = **+$1**; at expiry the (stock+put‚àícall) book is worth exactly K=95 and you repay 95 ‚áí **$0**."* Most-likely wrong gap: **`11`** (sign-flip `(C‚àíP)‚àí(K‚àíS)=6+5`) or **`6`** (stop at `C‚àíP`, forget `S‚àíK`). Assist (below) prefills the `S‚àíK` term so the beat never dead-ends.

**Misconceptions targeted:** **(a)** parity is an empirical approximation / needs a pricing model ‚Äî refuted: it's an exact no-arb identity, two same-payoff portfolios cost the same, no Black-Scholes; **(b)** sign-flip `C‚àíP=K‚àíS` ‚Äî refuted by the synthetic stock-minus-strike payoff (check S_T‚â•K and S_T<K); **(c)** "with r=0 the discount still matters" / mishandling D ‚Äî at r=0, D=1 so `C‚àíP=S‚àíK`, the discount enters **only** via `K¬∑D` (re-tested on the transfer where D=10/11 bites); **(d)** gate foil ‚Äî rebuild the replicating portfolio from scratch vs recognize the identity that already encodes it.
