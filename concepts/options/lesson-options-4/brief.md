## Hook (the bet)
The stock is 100. In one step it goes to **120** or **80**. A call struck at 100 therefore pays **20** or **0**. Quick — to price it, *which probability do you weight the payoffs by?* The learner reaches for the real-world chance the stock rises… and discovers they were never even **told** it. The astonishing fact this lesson delivers: **the price does not depend on the real up-probability at all.** A different weight `q=(R−d)/(u−d)`, forced purely by no-arbitrage, replaces it — and it is **not** a probability, **not** a Bayes posterior. That bait (price = average payoff under the *real* odds?) is the productive-failure seed, and killing it is the whole lesson.

## Core promise (one idea)
**You price an option by building the portfolio that copies it — Δ shares + a bond B — and the cost of that hedge is the price.** Equivalently, `price = (1/R)·E_q[payoff]` with the **risk-neutral weight** `q=(R−d)/(u−d)`. Replication and the q-measure are the *same* answer; the real-world probability never enters either one.

## EXACT-RATIONAL constraint (lesson-critical)
Every **graded** answer is the exact rational `src/engine/options.ts` reproduces — **never a float.** `u, d, R` are chosen as exact rationals so that the whole pipeline stays rational:

> `q=(R−d)/(u−d)` → `price=(1/R)·E_q[payoff]` → `Δ=(V_u−V_d)/(S(u−d))` → `B=price−Δ·S`, all ∈ ℚ.

- The **CRR parametrization** `u=e^(σ√Δt)`, `R=e^(rΔt)` is irrational and is **REJECTED as a graded input** — `u,d,R` enter as given rationals (`6/5,4/5,1`; `7/4,3/4,5/4`; `3/2,1/2,11/10`). The σ→u,d map is illustrative continuous-limit context only.
- Prefer **`r=0 ⇒ R=1`** (canonical #13/#16); otherwise a given exact-rational `R` (`5/4` for #14, `11/10` for #15).
- **Black-Scholes is L5 and DISPLAY-ONLY** here (`N(·)` is irrational); it never appears as an `accept` in this lesson. Same "grade the exact rational, never the transcendental/root" discipline as covariance grading **ρ²/Cov** instead of the irrational ρ.

## Misconceptions (Specialist) — per-option refutation copy, marquee first
This is the concept's **marquee misconception lesson**: the load-bearing error is that **q is the real probability / a Bayes posterior**. The four gate options each encode one mental model; the `byOption` copy below is the exact text a learner sees.

**① MARQUEE — "q = the real/physical up-probability"** *(seeded at the bet `opt4-bet`; refuted across the gate `opt4-gate`).* — q is a **no-arbitrage construct** `(R−d)/(u−d)`; the real up-probability **never enters the price**. The replicating hedge has the **same cost no matter the real odds** — you can price the call without ever being told how likely the stock is to rise.

**`opt4-bet` `prediction` (ungraded, byOption — "Which probability prices the call?")** — 3 options:
- *"The real-world probability p that the stock rises"* → **"Let's test it — but you were never told p. The astonishing result: the price doesn't depend on it. No-arbitrage forces a *different* weight `q=(R−d)/(u−d)` that replaces the real odds entirely (#13)."** (false)
- *"A weight q you compute from u, d, R (not p), then discount"* → **"Good instinct — price comes from replication / no-arbitrage. Here `q=(R−d)/(u−d)=1/2`, then discount by 1/R. The real p never appears (#13)."** (true)
- *"50/50 — by symmetry the two outcomes are equally likely"* → **"Let's test it — you land on 1/2, but for the wrong reason. It isn't symmetry of the real odds; it's `q=(R−d)/(u−d)`, which is 1/2 only for *this* tree. Change u,d,R and q moves — the q-drill gives 3/5 (#15)."** (false — the right number, the wrong object)

**`opt4-gate` `prediction.gate` (graded; the marquee discrimination)** — label-stripped prompt: *"A stock at 100 moves to 120 or 80 in one step; R=1. A call struck at 100 pays 20 or 0. Which method gives its arbitrage-free price?"* `correct = risk-neutral-pricing`. Options/`optionMethods` (positionally aligned, **all ⊂ `CONFUSABLE[risk-neutral-pricing]`**):

| option label | `optionMethod` | the wrong model it encodes → refutation `byOption` copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Risk-neutral pricing** | `risk-neutral-pricing` | **(correct)** *"Discount the q-weighted payoff: `q=(R−d)/(u−d)`, then `price=(1/R)·E_q`. q comes from no-arbitrage, not from the real odds — that's why you never needed p (#13)."* |
| Linearity / indicators | `linearity-indicators` | **"price = the (undiscounted) expected payoff under p"** → *"Linearity of expectation under the *real* p doesn't price an option — that's the marquee trap. You must switch to the no-arbitrage weight q **and** discount by 1/R. Averaging 20 and 0 under real odds is not the price (#13)."* |
| Conditioning | `conditioning` | **"it's just a first-step conditional average"** → *"Conditioning on the first step is the right *shape*, but only the **q-weighted, discounted** version is arbitrage-free. A plain conditional average under p still smuggles in the real odds (#13)."* |
| Prior update | `prior-update` | **"q is a Bayesian posterior / an updated belief"** → *"There is no prior and no evidence here. q is a **change of measure forced by no-arbitrage**, not Bayes' rule — you don't update a belief about the stock, you solve `(R−d)/(u−d)`. (The continuity report flags this exact false-analogy; #13.)"* |

**`opt4-win` hint ladder** (accept `1/2`): nudge *"q=(R−d)/(u−d); plug u=6/5, d=4/5, R=1"* → method *"(1−4/5)/(6/5−4/5) = (1/5)/(2/5)"* → answer *"**1/2** (#13)."* Most likely wrong: a stated real probability, or "1/2 by symmetry" (right value, exposed as wrong reasoning by #15's q=3/5).

**`opt4-mastery`** (accept q `1/2`, price `10`, Δ `1/2`, B `−40`): most likely wrong = pricing by `E_p` (uses a made-up p), forgetting the 1/R discount, or `B=+40` (sign error). Refutation closes on **hedge cost = ½·100 − 40 = 10 = price** — replication and the q-measure agree.

**Other misconceptions targeted:** "the option value ≠ its hedge cost" (refuted in `opt4-explore`/mastery — they are equal by no-arbitrage); "q is always 1/2" (refuted by #15, q=3/5, in the model on-ramp and the bet); "a put needs a different pricing method" (refuted in the held-out transfer — same q-method, then parity confirms C−P=0).
