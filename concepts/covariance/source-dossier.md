- **Problems verified: 13** (all pass both gates — answer stated by a source AND reproducible by a pure exact-rational engine). Each numeric answer was re-derived by hand below.
- **Hardest exact-rational risk:** ρ = Cov/√(Var_X·Var_Y) is generically irrational. The sharpest trap is the dice problem **corr(X₁, X₁+X₂) = 1/√2** and the **Jane Street 0.9/0.8 variant** (range 0.72 ± 0.6√0.19) — both irrational. Solution: the engine must store/grade **ρ² (always rational)** or the covariance/variance directly, never a float ρ. Selected only ρ-families whose √ collapses to a rational: √(1−0.8²)=0.6, √(1−0.6²)=0.8, the symmetry-zero case ρ=0, and the order-statistic case ρ=1/2.
- **Reformulated to stay rational:** the dice **corr(X₁, X₁+X₂)** is included **only as ρ²=1/2** (and as the exact covariance 35/12), with the irrational ρ=1/√2 explicitly flagged "display-only, never graded as a float." The Jane Street 0.9/0.8 range is **REJECTED** (see bottom); only the GB 0.8/0.8 twin survives.

## REJECTED problems (irrational answer, no rational reformulation → keep out of the graded set)
- **Jane Street "ρ(X,Y)=0.9, ρ(Y,Z)=0.8, range of ρ(X,Z)"** (Glassdoor / Jane Street, widely reproduced): range = 0.72 ± √(1−0.81)·√(1−0.64) = 0.72 ± 0.6√0.19. **√0.19 is irrational** and does not collapse — reject. (Use the GB 0.8/0.8 twin, problem #10, instead, where it collapses to 7/25 and 1.)
- **corr(X₁, X₁+X₂) for two dice = 1/√2** ([randomservices.org](https://www.randomservices.org/random/expect/Covariance.html)): irrational ρ. Included **only as ρ²=1/2 / Cov=35/12** (problems #3-#4) — never as the float 0.7071.
- **Pearson ρ of two general standard normals via a non-Pythagorean given ρ** (e.g. ρ=1/3 → √(1−1/9)=√8/3 irrational): any ρ-range problem whose inputs are not Pythagorean pairs (3/5,4/5 / 0.6,0.8) yields irrational √(1−ρ²). Reject unless reformulated through ρ² or PSD-determinant inequalities (as in #10-#12).
- **corr of two correlated normals from a regression slope with irrational R²** — reject; only include R² (=ρ²) variants.
