# Lesson Brief: Reversibility & Detailed Balance  (lesson-markov-chains-8)

## Hook  (the bet)

"Two urns, a handful of balls. Each tick you reach in, grab **one ball at random** — from *either* side — and move it across. Run it forever: what fraction of the time do you find the balls split right down the middle? You could set up `πP=π` and grind through L6's algebra all over again... **or** you could notice the urn has **no preferred direction** — every crossing is exactly as likely to happen as to undo — and read `π` straight off the picture. **The bet: can you get the long-run distribution *without solving the system?***"

## Core promise (one idea)

When a chain is **reversible** — when every edge balances its own back-flow, `πᵢpᵢⱼ=πⱼpⱼᵢ` (detailed balance) — you can build `π` edge by edge as a **telescoping product** instead of solving `πP=π`, and for a birth–death chain like the Ehrenfest urn that shortcut hands you `π(i)=C(m,i)/2ᵐ` for free.

> Exact-rational check (Stage-2 reproduces in `markov.ts` via `detailedBalance` / `isReversible` / `stationaryDistribution`; **no floats on any graded path**). Birth–death telescoping `πᵢ ∝ ∏ₖ<ᵢ (pₖ,ₖ₊₁/pₖ₊₁,ₖ)`. **m=2:** `π₀∝1`; `π₁∝p₀₁/p₁₀ = 1/(1/2) = 2`; `π₂∝π₁·p₁₂/p₂₁ = 2·(1/2)/1 = 1` ⇒ `(1,2,1)/4` = **(1/4,1/2,1/4)**. Single edge (0,1): `(1/4)·1 = π₁·(1/2) ⇒ π₁ = 1/2`. **m=3:** edge (0,1) `π₀·1=π₁·(1/3)⇒π₁=3π₀`; edge (1,2) `π₁·(2/3)=π₂·(2/3)⇒π₂=π₁=3π₀`; edge (2,3) `π₂·(1/3)=π₃·1⇒π₃=π₀` ⇒ `(1,3,3,1)/8` = **(1/8,3/8,3/8,1/8)** = `C(3,i)/2³`. **Directed 3-cycle:** stationary `(1/3,1/3,1/3)` exists (doubly stochastic), but `π_A p_AB = 1/3·1 = 1/3 ≠ π_B p_BA = 1/3·0 = 0` ⇒ detailed balance fails ⇒ **not reversible** (must fall back to L6's full `πP=π`).

## Misconceptions (Specialist)

- **"You always have to solve the full `πP=π` system to find `π`."** → fires at `guess-pi-bet` / `telescope-to-pi`. Refutation (`byOption`): *"Not for a reversible chain. If every edge balances — `πᵢpᵢⱼ=πⱼpⱼᵢ` — you can walk `π` out edge by edge as a telescoping product. On the Ehrenfest urn that's just `π ∝ (1,2,1)`, normalized to **(1/4,1/2,1/4)** — no linear system at all."*
- **"Detailed balance and stationarity are the same thing."** → fires at `reversible-or-not`. Refutation: *"Detailed balance ⇒ stationary, but **not** the reverse. The one-way cycle A→B→C→A is perfectly stationary at `(1/3,1/3,1/3)` — yet `π_A p_AB = 1/3` while the back-flow `π_B p_BA = 0`. It has a steady state but **no** edge balance. Reversibility is strictly stronger than stationarity."*
- **"Every chain is reversible, so the shortcut always works."** → fires at `reversible-or-not`. Refutation: *"Only chains whose forward flow equals back-flow on **every** edge. A one-way cycle obviously fails — run its movie backward and the traffic flows the wrong way. When detailed balance breaks, you drop back to L6's full `πP=π` solve."*
- **"In the Ehrenfest urn the balls settle to an even 50/50 split and stay there."** → fires at `ehrenfest-walk` / `mastery-ehrenfest-m3`. Refutation: *"`π` is **Binomial(m, ½)** — not a point mass at the middle and not uniform. m=2 gives **(1/4,1/2,1/4)** (the even split is most likely, but each extreme still carries 1/4); m=3 gives **(1/8,3/8,3/8,1/8)**. The middle is favored, the all-in-one-urn states are rare but never impossible."*
